Guideline ID: G001
Injury: Ankle Sprain
Treatment:
- RICE (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation) protocol for the first 48-72 hours.
- Pain relief using non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs).
- Physical therapy to restore range of motion, strength, and stability.
- In severe cases (Grade III sprain), a short-leg cast or walking boot for 2-3 weeks may be necessary.
- Follow-up with an orthopedic specialist for persistent symptoms.

Guideline ID: G002
Injury: Forearm Fracture
Treatment:
- Immediate immobilization of the arm to prevent further injury.
- X-ray to determine the location and severity of the fracture.
- For non-displaced fractures, a cast is typically applied for 6-8 weeks.
- For displaced fractures, surgical intervention involving plates, screws, or rods may be required to align and stabilize the bone.
- Post-cast removal, physical therapy is essential to regain strength and mobility.

Guideline ID: G003
Injury: Concussion
Treatment:
- Cognitive and physical rest is the cornerstone of treatment.
- Avoid activities that are physically demanding or require intense concentration.
- Gradually return to daily activities, including work and school.
- Monitor for worsening symptoms such as severe headache, repeated vomiting, or seizures, which require immediate medical attention.
- A follow-up with a neurologist may be recommended for complex cases.
